System Categorization

Types of Information Systems

Classifying the identified corporate software applications into their fundamental academic architectures based on operational and strategic functions.

HYBRID TPS MIS EIS

IT Asset Management System

Categorised as a comprehensive hybrid system. It functions as a Transaction Processing System (TPS) for daily operational transactions, acts as a Management Information System (MIS) for structured reporting, and serves as an Enterprise Information System (EIS) to provide executives with a macro-view of organizational asset health.

Operational, Middle & Executive Level
HYBRID TPS MIS

IFCA Application

Classified as a hybrid system serving the middle management and operational level by acting as both a Transaction Processing System (TPS) and a Management Information System (MIS).

Operational & Middle Management
HYBRID MIS ERP

Facilities Management System (FMS)

Functions primarily as a Management Information System (MIS) and incorporates Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP) capabilities to seamlessly track and integrate facility operations across the district.

Operational & Resource Planning Level
HYBRID HRIMS TPS MIS OAS

Employee Self System (ESS)

A comprehensive hybrid system combining a Human Resource Management Information System (HRIMS), TPS, MIS, and an Office Automation System (OAS) to digitize standard workflows and financial employee claims.

Multi-Level Integration
ADVANCED HYBRID TPS IoT MIS EIS AI

TRX Integrated Management System

A robust, advanced framework combining a Transaction Processing System (TPS), smart Internet of Things (IoT) technologies, a Management Information System (MIS), an Enterprise Information System (EIS), and Artificial Intelligence (AI) for predictive analysis, real-time monitoring, and holistic district coordination at the highest strategic levels.

Academic Application

This categorization clearly demonstrates the successful implementation of the IMS552 syllabus within a real-world corporate environment. By analyzing TRX City's software ecosystem, our team was able to practically identify, classify, and understand the distinct operational and strategic layers of different Information Systems.
